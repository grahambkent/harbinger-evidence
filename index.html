Fatal Fists V: Who Spiked the Punch?  ·  Production Office Memorandum
CONFIDENTIAL
INTERNAL USE ONLY
TO:Ronnie Feck, Director
FROM:B.J. Grant, Executive Producer
DATE:September 14, 2000
RE:Sc. 68 — Initiation Sequence / Tournament Finale
CC:Carla Denholm, 1st AD

Ronnie —

Want to get something in writing before we shoot the tournament finale sequence next week. I've been watching the dailies from earlier in the block and I think we're leaving something on the table.

The Rock Paper Scissors scenes need to feel visceral and real. Fang needs to be visibly uncomfortable — genuine tension, genuine sweat. The audience has to believe this man is fighting for his life, that the stakes are physical, not just dramatic. If it reads staged in the finale we lose the whole third act. I've seen it happen.

I know you've got your approach and I trust it. But I want us aligned on this before we roll. Whatever it takes to get our actor into that physiological state — authentic discomfort, authentic effort — that's what we're after. Real distress reads on camera in a way that performance never quite does. You know that better than anyone.

I'm making some scheduling adjustments to help with this. Carla has the updated one-line. Let's make this one count.

B.J. Grant
Executive Producer, Knife Nunchuck Productions
